Roughly a third of credit reports contain an error. Clearing inaccurate, unverifiable, or outdated items can move your score quickly. The rest of the climb to 800 is behavior: on-time payments, low balances, and patience. This desk handles both.

%What actually moves a FICO score

!The four golden rules

  • Dispute the truth, not your debts. Only challenge items that are actually wrong, unverifiable, mixed-up, duplicated, or past the legal reporting window. Disputing accurate information is temporary — it returns once verified — and knowingly making false statements can be fraud.
  • Identity-theft blocks (§605B) are only for real identity theft. That block requires a sworn FTC or police report. Filing a false one is a federal crime. Never claim fraud on an account you actually opened.
  • Habits are the real 800 engine. Pay every bill on time, keep card balances under 30% (then under 10%), keep old accounts open, and space out new applications.
  • You never have to pay for this. Under the Credit Repair Organizations Act, no company can charge you upfront or promise a specific score. Everything here is your free, do-it-yourself right.

↑When they stonewall — escalate

If a bureau "verifies" an item with no real proof, or a furnisher keeps reporting after a dispute, that may itself violate the FCRA. This is where most stubborn items finally come off.

1

Choose "Credit reporting" → the bureau or company. Companies are generally required to respond, usually within 15 days.

⚖Maryland escalation

  • Maryland Attorney General — Consumer Protection Division. File at marylandattorneygeneral.gov/Pages/CPD. Powerful against in-state collectors and furnishers under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act.
  • Maryland Commissioner of Financial Regulation. Check whether a debt buyer is licensed (labor.maryland.gov/finance). Under the Maryland Collection Agency Licensing Act, a debt buyer collecting a defaulted claim it bought generally must be licensed — and consumers have a private right of action (LVNV Funding LLC v. Finch).
  • Statute of limitations. Most consumer debt in Maryland has a 3-year suit limit (4 for sale of goods). A collector can still report within FCRA windows, but suing on time-barred debt is restricted, and a payment after the limit no longer revives it (Cts. & Jud. Proc. §5-1202).
  • Free legal help. Maryland Volunteer Lawyers Service and Pro Bono Resource Center handle credit and debt cases. For willful FCRA violations you can recover damages plus attorney's fees (§§1681n–1681o).

§Your toolkit — in plain English

These are the laws every letter on this desk leans on. Cite them by name and section; it signals you know your rights.

Federal — Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. §1681)

§609 · 15 U.S.C. §1681g — Your file disclosure
You can demand everything in your file and the source of each item. Good for seeing exactly what they have on you.
§611 · 15 U.S.C. §1681i — Reinvestigation
When you dispute, the bureau must do a reasonable reinvestigation within 30 days (45 if you add info), forward your dispute to the furnisher within 5 business days, and delete anything it cannot verify. You can also demand the method of verification (§611(a)(7)).
§623 · 15 U.S.C. §1681s-2 — Furnisher duties
The company reporting the debt must investigate disputes and may not keep furnishing information it knows is inaccurate. This is how you go straight to the source.
§605 · 15 U.S.C. §1681c — Obsolete information
Most negatives must drop off after 7 years; collections/charge-offs after 7 years + 180 days from the first delinquency; bankruptcies after 10 years. Older than that = must be removed.
§605B · 15 U.S.C. §1681c-2 — Identity-theft block
With a valid identity-theft report, the bureau must block fraudulent items within 4 business days. Powerful — and reserved strictly for genuine fraud.
§607(b) · 15 U.S.C. §1681e(b) — Maximum possible accuracy
Bureaus must use reasonable procedures to assure maximum possible accuracy. Sloppy, automated "verification" can violate this.
§616–617 · 15 U.S.C. §§1681n–1681o — Damages
Willful violations: actual or statutory damages of $100–$1,000, possible punitive damages, plus attorney's fees. Negligent: actual damages plus fees.

Federal — Fair Debt Collection Practices Act

§809 · 15 U.S.C. §1692g — Debt validation
Dispute within 30 days of a collector's first contact and they must stop collecting until they validate the debt in writing.

Maryland

MD Consumer Debt Collection Act — Com. Law §14-201 et seq.
Bans abusive, deceptive, and unfair collection. You can sue for violations.
MD Consumer Protection Act — Com. Law §13-301 et seq.
Covers deceptive practices, including false credit reporting by in-state companies.
MD Collection Agency Licensing Act — Bus. Reg. Title 7
Debt buyers collecting defaulted debt they purchased generally must be licensed. Unlicensed activity is challengeable (LVNV Funding LLC v. Finch).
Statute of limitations — Cts. & Jud. Proc. §5-101, §5-1202
3 years for most consumer debt (4 for sale of goods). Suing on time-barred debt is restricted; a late payment doesn't revive an expired debt.

Consumer protection

Credit Repair Organizations Act — 15 U.S.C. §1679
No one can charge you upfront or promise a specific result to "repair" credit. Everything here is free to do yourself.
